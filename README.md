# Employee-Attrition
Performed exploratory data analysis on employee attrition dataset , Identified key factors affecting attrition such as income, job role, tenure, overtime, and job satisfaction. Visualized trends to support HR decision-making

Objectives
Understand patterns and trends in employee attrition
Identify factors contributing to high attrition
Compare attrition across departments, job roles, and experience levels
Provide actionable insights for HR strategy

Key Insights
Attrition is highest among employees with low income and fewer salary hikes
Employees in the early years (1â€“3 years) show higher attrition
Overtime and poor work-life balance significantly increase attrition risk
Job satisfaction has a stronger impact on attrition than performance rating
Attrition varies significantly across job roles and departments

Conclusion
Employee attrition is influenced more by work environment, compensation, and job satisfaction than performance alone. Early identification of at-risk employees can help organizations improve retention and reduce operational costs.
